What is a Force?

A force is a push or pull that makes something move, stop, or change direction. Forces can be strong or weak, and they are present all around us.

  • Friction ‚Äì A force that slows things down when two objects rub against each other (like when you slide on the floor).

What is Friction?

Friction is a force that happens when two objects rub against each other. It slows things down or stops them from moving.

Examples of friction:

  • When you slide on the floor, you stop because of friction.
  • Car tires grip the road because of friction.
  • Rubbing your hands together creates heat because of friction.

Friction can be helpful (like helping shoes grip the ground) or unhelpful (like making it harder to push something heavy).

Examples of Simple Machines

Simple machines make work easier by using forces. There are six types of simple machines:

  • Lever ‚Äì A bar that pivots on a point (e.g., a seesaw, scissors).
  • Pulley ‚Äì A wheel with a rope used to lift objects (e.g., flagpoles, cranes).
  • Wheel and Axle ‚Äì A wheel attached to a rod that helps things move (e.g., cars, bicycles).
  • Inclined Plane ‚Äì A slanted surface that makes lifting easier (e.g., ramps).
  • Screw ‚Äì A twisted inclined plane that holds things together (e.g., a jar lid, screws in wood).
  • Wedge ‚Äì A pointed tool used to cut or split objects (e.g., a knife, an axe).

What is Pneumatic Technology?

Pneumatic technology uses compressed air or gas to create movement or force. It is used in many machines to make work easier.

Examples of pneumatic technology:

  • Air brakes in buses and trucks ‚Äì These use compressed air to stop vehicles.
  • Dentist drills ‚Äì The high-speed drill at the dentist uses air pressure to work.
  • Jackhammers ‚Äì These break up concrete using powerful air pressure.
  • Tires ‚Äì Car and bicycle tires are filled with air to keep them strong and bouncy.
